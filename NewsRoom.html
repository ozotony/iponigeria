    Online Registration of Trademark, Patent and Designs from the July 16, 2012.

    In its commitment to providing prompt and excellent service delivery consistent with modern innovation and technology, the Federal Ministry of Trade and Investment, recently engaged an indigenous Technology Consultant to automate itsTrademark, Patent and Designs (TP&D) Registry. This is to ensure convenience and efficiency in its operations, as well as discourage corrupt practices, necessitated by the cumbersome nature of the old process. Consequently, payment and registration of Trademark, Patent and Designs have been brought to the door step of every Stakeholder, as these could now be done at the touch of the button within the comfort of the office space, home, and everywhere.

    The automation process was preceded by months of data/requirements gathering, study of the manual process, provision of technology equipment, and capacity building at various levels imaginable.

    The Department commenced a pilot scheme on May 11, 2012 and invited a cross-section of Stakeholders to participate and make their input on the new system. In addition to this, available staffs were individually and collectively assessed in order to ensure proper deployment to areas of the registry, where they could function more effectively and efficiently.By and large, the co-operation of all staff and stakeholders has shown their commitment to the project, especially as touching upon improvement on service delivery.

    Operationally, the procedure opens with the engagement of an accredited Agent of the Registry, who is expected to provide necessary professional advice on the requirements needed for filing Trademark, Patent and Designs in Nigeria. Some of the criteria that would be required by the Agent for registration include; information on the Trademark, Patent and Design, applicant’s information and power of attorney, as well as appropriate fees payable at a designated bank, using online-generated invoices. While it is expected that other banks shall embrace the programme much later, Skye Bank Plc has been chosen for the pilot.

    The Registry is expected to officially start off the online Registration of Trademark, Patent and Designs from the July 16, 2012. With this, the use of Bank draft and manual method of Registration will cease, as payment for Registration will now be done online.

    It should be noted that Data capture is on-going in order to ensure that old applications and files are captured into the system for easy real-time online search. It should also be added that the search office has however been partially automated in order to ensure the smooth running of the online system. The benefits of the system are:

    >>E-Payment of fees,

    >>Filing applications using online forms,

    >>Online publication of Trademarks, Patent and Designs,

    >>Reduced process time of applications,

    >>Security certificate documents,

    >>Professionalism in delivery of service, as well as

    >>More effective and efficient service delivery.

    In conclusion, this new project is expected to raise the profile of Nigeria in the area of Intellectual Property protection, in a way consistent with best practices across the globe. It is therefore expected that all relevant Stakeholders should ensure a quick buy-in by getting accredited using the online procedure as directed.
